Ashwagandha

Tonic and adaptogen that has 4,000 years of traditional use in its native India, Pakistan, and Sri Lanka. Ashwagandha helps your body cope with stress, enhances focus and stamina, and reduces irritability.

Chamomile

Gentle herb known throughout the world as a calmer of spirit, soother of the digestive tract, and friend to children. Chamomile is featured in the Ebers Papyrus, an ancient Egyptian text considered one of the greatest medical documents in recorded history. The Tzeltal Maya of Mexico make a chamomile tea containing orange and lime leaf to uplift the mood. Germans refer to chamomile as alles zutraut meaning `capable of anything.`

Make a tea from dried flowers before bed or anytime you need a mood lift. Plays well with dried lavender, rose, and milk oats.

Lavender

Popular herb for creating a sense of peace. One of the most recognized scents in the world. Acts on the part of the brain (the amygdala) that regulates emotions and memory.

Drop a few drops of lavender oil on a piece of cloth, close your eyes, and breathe in. Create a diy steam room by adding a few drops of oil to the bottom of your shower. Make a sleep mask with dried flowers and organic linen. Steep dried flowers in boiling water for a calming tea. Plays well with chamomile, rose, and oatstraw.
