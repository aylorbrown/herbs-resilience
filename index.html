Ashwagandha

Tonic and adaptogen that has 4,000 years of traditional use in its native India, Pakistan, and Sri Lanka. Well studied and beloved for lowering cortisol levels and helping your body cope with stress. Ashwagandha rejuvenates and tonifies your entire system, especially the endocrine and immune systems.

Add the ground powder to morning tea or make a soothing latte with non-dairy milk, turmeric, ginger, cardamom and ½ tsp of ashwagandha.

Chamomile

Gentle herb known throughout the world as a calmer of spirit, soother of the digestive tract, and friend to children. The Tzeltal Maya of Mexico make a chamomile tea containing orange and lime leaf to uplift the mood. Germans refer to chamomile as alles zutraut meaning `capable of anything.`

Make a tea from dried flowers before bed or anytime you need a mood lift. Plays well with dried lavender, rose, milk oats, and skullcap.

Ginger

Immune-supporting friend to all. Ginger is one of the most widely consumed aromatic spices on the planet so if you're not cooking with ginger and garlic wyd? This -- root is used medicinally to calm nausea, restore appetite, and aid in a healthy immune respiratory response.

Slice a palm-sized amount of fresh ginger root and steep it in boiling water for tea. Make a broth with garlic, ginger, carrots and chicken.

Lavender

Popular herb for creating a sense of peace. One of the most recognized scents in the world. Acts on the part of the brain (the amygdala) that regulates emotions and memory.

Drop a few drops of lavender oil on a piece of cloth, close your eyes, and breathe in. Create a diy steam room by adding a few drops of oil to the bottom of your shower. Make a sleep mask with dried flowers and organic linen. Steep dried flowers in boiling water for a calming tea. Plays well with chamomile, rose, and oatstraw.

Skullcap

Supports the health of the nervous system and promotes a healthy response to stress. Active compounds in skullcap bind to the same receptor sites in our brains as benzodiazepines (meaning, you can think of it as nature’s Xanax). While other comforting herbs like chamomile may make you sleepy, skullcap soothes the nerves without slowing you down.
